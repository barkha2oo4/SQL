Pizza Sales Analysis Project

This project focuses on analyzing pizza sales data to uncover key business insights using SQL.
The analysis covers total orders, revenue generation, sales patterns by time, customer preferences for pizza types and sizes, and revenue distribution across categories.
It is designed to enhance data-driven decision-making for restaurant operations and marketing strategies.
📂 Dataset

The dataset consists of multiple tables:
- **orders**: Order ID, date, and time.
- **order_details**: Items per order (pizza_id, quantity).
- **pizzas**: Pizza details (type, size, price).
- **pizza_types**: Category (Veg/Non-Veg, etc.).

💡 Business Questions Answered
🔹 Basic Queries
- Total number of orders placed.
- Total revenue generated from pizza sales.
- Highest-priced pizza on the menu.
- Most common pizza size ordered.
- Top 5 most-ordered pizza types.
🔹 Intermediate Queries
- Quantity sold by pizza category (e.g., Classic, Veggie).
- Order distribution by hour of the day.
- Category-wise pizza distribution.
- Average pizzas ordered per day.
- Top 3 pizza types by revenue.
🔹 Advanced Queries
- % Revenue contribution by each pizza type.
- Cumulative revenue trend over time.
- Top 3 pizza types by revenue **per category**.

🛠️ Tools & Technologies

- **SQL** (Joins, Aggregations, Window Functions)
- **MySQL Workbench** (or your SQL editor)

📈 Key Insights

- **Margherita** is the top-selling pizza, contributing ~15% to total revenue.
- Peak order times are around **7–9 PM**, indicating strong dinner sales.
- **Classic** pizzas dominate sales, but **Gourmet** pizzas drive higher revenue per order.
- The **Large** size is the most popular, accounting for 60% of orders.
Conclusion

The pizza sales analysis provided valuable insights into customer behavior, peak ordering times, and top-performing pizza types.
Key findings like the dominance of large-sized pizzas, revenue concentration among a few premium pizzas, and peak sales during evening hours can help the business optimize inventory, promotions, and staffing.
Using data-driven strategies, the restaurant can significantly enhance customer satisfaction and boost overall revenue.
